Some financial-related definition

- Asset: Anything of value owned by an individual, organization, or company that can be converted into cash.
- Liability: Financial obligations or debts that a person, business, or entity owes to others.
- Equity: The ownership interest in a company, represented by shares, or the residual interest after subtracting liabilities from assets.
- Income: The money received by an individual or business from various sources, such as wages, salaries, investments, or sales.
- Expense: The cost incurred or money spent in order to generate revenue or maintain operations.
- Budget: A financial plan that outlines expected income and expenses over a specific period, often used for managing personal or business finances.
- Revenue: The total income generated by a business from its normal activities, such as sales of goods or services.
- Profit: The financial gain realized when the revenue generated exceeds the expenses incurred.
- Loss: The negative financial result that occurs when expenses exceed revenue.
- Cash Flow: The movement of money into and out of a business, representing the net amount of cash generated or consumed during a specific period.
- Interest: The cost of borrowing money or the compensation received for lending money, expressed as a percentage of the principal amount.
- Principal: The original amount of money invested or loaned, excluding any interest or additional charges.
- Investment: The allocation of resources, typically money, with the expectation of generating income or profit in the future.
- Stock: A type of security representing ownership in a company, typically divided into shares that can be bought, sold, or traded on the stock market.
- Bond: A debt investment where an investor lends money to a government or corporation for a defined period at a fixed interest rate.
- Diversification: A risk management strategy that involves spreading investments across different asset classes, industries, or geographical regions to reduce exposure to any single asset or risk.
- Portfolio: A collection of investments held by an individual or institution, including stocks, bonds, mutual funds, and other assets.
- Return on Investment (ROI): A measure used to evaluate the profitability of an investment, calculated as the ratio of net profit or gain to the initial investment amount.
- Inflation: The rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services rises over time, eroding purchasing power.
- Depreciation: The decrease in value of an asset over time, reflecting factors such as wear and tear, obsolescence, or market conditions.
